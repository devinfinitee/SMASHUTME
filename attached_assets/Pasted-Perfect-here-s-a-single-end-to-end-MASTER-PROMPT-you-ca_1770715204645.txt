# 🚀 MASTER PROJECT PROMPT

### SmashUTME — Replit-Inspired EdTech Platform for UTME Preparation

---

## ROLE & CONTEXT

You are a **senior product designer + full-stack architect** working on an **open-source–friendly EdTech web platform**, inspired by the **structure, clarity, and modularity of Replit**, but adapted for **UTME/JAMB exam preparation in Nigeria**.

The product is called **SmashUTME**.

The goal is to build a **scalable, student-first learning platform** that helps UTME candidates:

* Study the official JAMB syllabus
* Focus on **high-yield topics**
* Practice **JAMB past questions**
* Get **AI-assisted explanations** when stuck
* Learn collaboratively through groups

The platform should be **simple, fast, mobile-first**, and usable even with **low bandwidth**.

---

## PRODUCT VISION

SmashUTME is **not just a reading app**.
It is a **guided learning workspace**, similar in philosophy to Replit:

* Clear structure
* Progressive learning
* Intelligent assistance
* User personalization
* Strong defaults
* Minimal cognitive overload

AI is used **surgically**, not as a free chat.

---

## TARGET USERS

* UTME candidates (SS3, drop-year students)
* Nigerian students using mobile devices
* Limited data, intermittent internet
* High exam pressure, short attention spans

---

## CORE PRINCIPLES (VERY IMPORTANT)

* **Curriculum-first, AI-second**
* **High-yield over volume**
* **Guided flows, not free exploration**
* **Static content first, AI as a helper**
* **Everything personalized to the student**
* **Graceful degradation if AI is unavailable**

---

## FEATURE SCOPE (MVP-FOCUSED)

### 1. Authentication & Onboarding (Replit-style guided setup)

* User creates account (email / Google)
* First-time onboarding wizard:

  * UTME year
  * Subjects combination (e.g. English, Biology, Chemistry, Physics)
  * Weak vs strong subjects
  * Daily study goal (minutes)
* Preferences are saved and used to:

  * Filter content
  * Recommend topics
  * Control quizzes

---

### 2. Dashboard (Main Workspace)

Replit-inspired layout:

* Left sidebar: Subjects
* Main panel: Current focus
* Right panel (optional): Progress / tips

Dashboard shows:

* Today’s study plan
* High-yield topics to cover
* Recently viewed topics
* Quick “Practice Questions” button
* Progress indicators per subject

---

### 3. Subjects & Topics Structure

Each subject page contains:

* Full list of topics from **official JAMB syllabus**
* Topics grouped into:

  * High-Yield Topics
  * Regular Topics
* Progress indicator per topic:

  * Not started
  * In progress
  * Completed

---

### 4. Topic Reading Page (Core Learning Page)

This is the **most important page**.

Layout:

* Clean reading interface
* Clear headings
* Bullet points
* Diagrams / tables where necessary
* UTME-focused explanations (concise, exam-oriented)

On the page:

* Topic explanation (CMS-driven)
* Key points summary
* Common UTME traps
* “Practice Questions from this topic”

---

### 5. AI Assistant (Modal-based, NOT free chat)

Inspired by Replit’s inline AI help.

AI appears as a **modal or side panel**, triggered by buttons such as:

* “Explain this simply”
* “Give a UTME example”
* “Why is this important for UTME?”
* “Explain why option B is wrong”

Constraints:

* No open-ended chat
* Short responses
* Cached per topic
* Daily limits per user

AI provider: **Gemini API**

---

### 6. Quiz & Practice System

* Topic-based quizzes
* Subject-based quizzes
* UTME-style CBT simulation
* Timed questions
* Immediate feedback
* Explanation shown after answering

Quiz bot logic:

* Weak areas prioritized
* Repeated mistakes flagged
* Performance stored per topic

---

### 7. Groups & Collaboration (Lightweight)

* Join subject-based or school-based groups
* Discussion threads
* Brainstorming on difficult questions
* Moderated to avoid spam

---

### 8. Admin CMS (Replit-style control panel)

Admin can:

* Create subjects
* Create topics
* Mark topics as high-yield
* Upload notes
* Upload questions & answers
* Manage AI pre-generated explanations

CMS should be:

* Simple
* Structured
* Versionable (future open-source)

---

## NON-FUNCTIONAL REQUIREMENTS

* Mobile-first UI
* Fast load times
* Works on low-end Android devices
* Minimal animations
* Offline-friendly (future support)
* Graceful AI failure handling

## TECHNICAL ARCHITECTURE (INSPO, NOT CODE)

* Backend: Node.js / Express or similar
